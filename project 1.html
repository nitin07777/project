INDIA

A flag is a symbol that represents a country, organisation or idea. it is usually made of cloth and has a specific design of colors, shapes and symbols that hold deep meaning. every nation has it's own unique flag that reflect it's history, culture and values. for example, the indian national flag, also known as the **Tiranga**, has three horizontal stripes of saffron, white and green. in the centre is a navy blue Ashoka Chakra with 24 spokes. each color of th flag has it's own meaning: saffron represents courage and sacrifice, white stands for peace and truth and green symbolises faith and prosperity. the Ashoka Chakra signifies the eternal wheel of law. people show great respect and pride for their national flag, especially on important occasion like **Independence day** and **Republic day**. the flag is hoisted with honor, and citizens often sing the national anthem while saluting it. it serves as a reminder of the struggles and sacrifices made by freedom fighters. the flag also unites people of different cultures and backgrounds under the identity. in this way, a flag is not just a piece of fabric it is a symbol of unity, pride and national identity.
